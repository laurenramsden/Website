Background

I am a Sri-Lankan born British citizen who feld the country and immigrated to England in the early 2000's. For those who haven't visited, would highly recommend- it's a beautiful country!!

I have two older brothers, so was practically a human punching bag. Nothing out of the ordinary with them; constant fights over the TV remote and then me being the princess I am playing the youngest card and getting what I wanted as soon as I shed a tear.

Lack of brain power has caused me to forget most of my childhood, but one thing that forever etched in my memory was both my brothers and I breaking our left arms all in the space of a year, for various reasons.

As for my parents, both the traditional Asian parents with endless siblings, leaving me with more than enough cousins and aunties that I to this day still dont know how they are related. Still have to speak in Tamil to them becuase appararenlty 17 years in the country doesnt do muh for vocab. Fun fact actually I'm the frst person in my immediate family to speak English as my first language- not that it stops my university friends from discriminating against my London lingo.

Anyways, from my 19 years of precious life, apart from the did duke of edinburgh, goes to uiversity etc, my biggest achievement and experience has to be Camp America so here's a little summary of it all...

Camp Sealth, Seattle

camp name: chilli

Eight weeks looking after children on a coastside camp on paper seems like a dream. Joke was on me because never have I appreciated my mum so much- kids are no easy ride. It was an emoional rollercoaster working with kids with diabetes to those on the ADHD spectrum but rewarding doesn't even begin to describe how I felt afterwards. Not to mention the lifeong friends you make from across the world, as camp is mostly Americans, but 30% interntaionals. Best believe if I am to visit Australia I will be using my friends for free accommodation. Anyways heres a few cute photos for y'all
